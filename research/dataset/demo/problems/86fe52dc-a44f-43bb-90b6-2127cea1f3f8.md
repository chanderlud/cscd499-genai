
# Time-Rewind Courier (Rust)

A courier robot must deliver a “spell” (a string) by traversing a directed graph. Some edges consume the next character of the spell, others are portals that move without consuming anything. The catch: entering certain rooms rewinds time and **reduces your progress** through the spell.

Your job is to compute:

1) The minimum number of steps required to deliver the full spell and end at a target room.  
2) The number of distinct minimum-step walks that achieve that, modulo **1_000_000_007**.

---

## World Model

There are `n` rooms labeled `0..n-1`.

There are two kinds of directed edges:

### 1) Roads (consume a character)
Each road is `(from, to, label)` where `label` is a lowercase ASCII letter `'a'..'z'`.

If you are at room `u` with progress `i` (meaning the next character to deliver is `spell[i]`), you may traverse a road `(u, v, c)` **only if** `i < spell.len()` and `c == spell[i]`.

- After traversing the road, progress becomes `i + 1`.
- Then **upon entering** room `v`, a rewind (if any) is applied.

### 2) Portals (do not consume a character)
Each portal is `(from, to)`.

You may traverse a portal regardless of progress.

- After traversing the portal, progress stays `i`.
- Then **upon entering** room `v`, a rewind (if any) is applied.

### Rewinds (applied on entry)
Some rooms have a rewind amount `k >= 0`. Represented as pairs `(room, k)`.

Whenever you **enter** a room `r`, after the edge’s consume/non-consume effect, you apply:

- `progress = progress.saturating_sub(k)`

Important: rewinds apply **only on entry**. The starting position does **not** apply a rewind automatically.

---

## Objective

You start at:

- room `start`
- progress `0`

You succeed only if you reach the state:

- room `goal`
- progress exactly `spell.len()`

(Meaning the spell is fully delivered **after** any rewind on entering `goal`.)

All edges (roads and portals) cost **exactly 1 step**.

---

## Function Signature (Rust)

Implement:

```rust
pub fn time_rewind_courier(
    n: usize,
    roads: &[(usize, usize, char)],
    portals: &[(usize, usize)],
    rewinds: &[(usize, usize)],
    start: usize,
    goal: usize,
    spell: &str,
) -> (i32, i64)
````

Return:

* `(min_steps, ways)`
* If impossible, return `(-1, 0)`
* `ways` must be modulo `1_000_000_007`

Distinct walks are distinct sequences of traversed edges (so taking a different intermediate room counts as different).

---

## Constraints

Design your solution to handle:

* `1 <= n <= 3000`
* `0 <= roads.len() <= 20000`
* `0 <= portals.len() <= 20000`
* `0 <= spell.len() <= 2000`
* `0 <= k <= spell.len()` for rewinds

Expected approach: shortest path on a product graph of states `(room, progress)` with counting shortest paths.

---

## Notes / Edge Cases

* Empty spell (`spell.len() == 0`) means you are already “delivered” at progress 0, but you still must be at `goal`.
* A rewind on `goal` can make delivery impossible even if you “consume” the last letter on the final move.
* Rewinds can cause revisiting earlier progress indices; do not assume monotonic progress.
